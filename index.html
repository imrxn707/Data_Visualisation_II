Mapping Malaysia's Foreign Arrivals

An Insight into Foreign Arrivals into Malaysia (2020 - 2023)

The global pandemic has drastically altered international travel, and Malaysia saw a massive decline in foreign arrivals. As the world recovered, international travels are returning, revealing intriguing trends. This visualisation aims to provide a comprehensive overview of foreign arrivals into Malaysia between 2020 and 2023.

What can this data tell us about Malaysia's tourism revival, and what does the future hold for global travel?

Let's explore the story behind the numbers.

Entry points into Malaysia

The proportional symbol map illustrates the key points of entry into Malaysia, highlighting where foreign arrivals are most concentrated. The largest symbols on the map represent the busiest entry points, with Bangunan Sultan Iskandar in Johor leading as the top entry location, followed closely by Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA) and its two terminals.

Bangunan Sultan Iskandar experiences the highest traffic due to its strategic location at the Malaysia-Singapore border. Every day, thousands of Malaysians and Singaporeans cross between the two countries for work, leisure, and trade, making it one of the busiest land crossings in the world. Meanwhile, KLIA, as the nation’s main international gateway, naturally handles a vast amount of air traffic, connecting Malaysia to major global destinations and serving as a key hub for international travelers.

Arrival Trends: Changes Over the Years

The chart above depicts the trends in foreign arrivals into Malaysia from 2020 to 2023, seperated by gender. It clearly shows the significant decline in arrivals at the start of 2020, coinciding with the global COVID-19 pandemic. By mid-2020, arrivals had dropped to almost zero due to international travel restrictions and border closures.

However, starting from early 2022, we see a gradual recovery as Malaysia reopens its borders and international travel begins to pick up again. The trend shows consistent growth, with both male and female arrivals increasing steadily. By late 2023, arrivals reach a stable point, reflecting the normalization of travel post-pandemic.

Number of Arrivals by State

This bar chart shows the number of foreign arrivals into Malaysia, broken down by state. Johor stands out with the highest number of arrivals, far surpassing other states. This is largely due to Johor’s close proximity to Singapore, one of the busiest international land crossings in the world. Many Malaysians and Singaporeans frequently travel between the two countries for work, business, and leisure, contributing to Johor's overwhelming dominance in arrivals.

Selangor, which includes Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA), ranks second in terms of arrivals. As the main international gateway for air travelers into Malaysia, it is a major hub for tourists, business travelers, and international connections.

States like Sarawak, Pulau Pinang, and Kedah show significantly lower arrival numbers due to smaller international entry points, limited air traffic, and geographical factors. These states also tend to attract more domestic than international tourists, leading to fewer foreign arrivals compared to Johor and Selangor.
